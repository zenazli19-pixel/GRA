Slurp-Worthy Drunken Noodles

From our dining room to yours

Drunken Noodles is a vibrant Thai street food classic that balances spice, sweetness, and savory depth in every bite. The dish is made with wide rice noodles, stir-fried vegetables, and aromatic basil leaves that create a symphony of flavor. Its bold taste has made it a favorite around the world and especially in our kitchen. If you're looking for confort food with a kick, this dish never disappoints.

  • Prep Time:
    15 mins
  • Cook Time:
    10 mins
  • Servings:
    2-3 servings

Ingredients

  • 8 oz wide rice noodes (fresh or dried)
  • 2 tbs vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1-2 Thai bird's eye chilies, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup red bell peppers sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli or Chinese broccoli
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 3 tbs soy sauce
  • 1 tbs dark soy sauce
  • 2 tbs oyser sauce
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Directions

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water until pliable, then drain.
  2. Mix soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar in a bowl
  3. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat.
  4. Add garlic and chilies: stir-fry until fragrant.
  5. Stir in onions, bell peppers, an broccoli. Cook until tender-crisp
  6. Add noodles and stir-fry quickly.
  7. Pour in the sauce and stir until noodles are coated
  8. Toss in Thai basil leaves until wilted.
  9. Serve hot with lime wedges
  10. Enjoy!
